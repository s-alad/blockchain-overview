What is it really?

Blockchain technology is often misunderstood and used interchangeably with Cryptocurrency or other forms of adoption. In the most simple terms, Blockchain technology is just a system of distribution. The earliest proposal of blockchain technology comes from a dissertation by David L Chaum, A doctor at UCLA, where it is stated as "a distributed computer system that can be established, maintained, and trusted by mutually suspicious groups." It is a distributed database where each record of data can be stored and verified across the network without having to rely on a centralized authority. The fact that there is no central authority in blockchain technology makes it very appealing to many people, as the data can be made more private, secure, and anonymous than if controlled by a central authority. Blockchain, in its essence, is just a logistical system of decentralized distribution; it is just a blueprint on how to deal with data that has traditionally been controlled and verified by a central authority (generally a company, government, or third party). Blockchain in itself is a harmless mathematical and logistical system that allows for decentralization and consistency.
